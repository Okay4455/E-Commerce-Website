Technology

Technology has had a profound impact on the modern world and has revolutionized almost every aspect of our daily lives. From the way we communicate, work, learn, entertain ourselves, and even access information, technology has made it all faster, more convenient, and more accessible. The rise of the internet and mobile devices has made it possible for people to connect with one another from anywhere in the world. Artificial intelligence and machine learning are changing the way businesses operate and providing new solutions to age-old problems. The use of technology has also increased efficiency, productivity and has led to the creation of new job opportunities. However, with the rapid advancement of technology also comes the need for individuals and society as a whole to adapt to and understand its effects on privacy, security, and ethics.

Positive affects of technologies in modern society

Improved Communication

Technology has made communication faster, more efficient and more accessible, allowing people to connect with each other from anywhere in the world with access to communicate face-to-face. Improved communication can lead to better understanding, stronger relationships and increased efficiency in personal and professional settings. It involves active listening, clear expression, empathy and the use of appropriate non-verbal cues. Regular communication skills training and open-mindedness can enhance one's ability to communicate effectively.

Increased Access to Information

The Internet and other digital technologies have made vast amounts of information readily available to people, allowing them to learn and grow and have enough access to information in ways that were not possible before. Increased access to information can lead to better decision making, increased knowledge, and empowerment. With the rise of technology, access to information has become easier than ever before through the internet and digital devices. However, it's important to critically evaluate the sources and verify the accuracy of the information obtained.

Improved Quality of Life

Technology has made many aspects of daily life more convenient and easier, from shopping and paying bills to transportation and home automation. Improved quality of life means an enhanced standard of living and overall well-being. It can be achieved through a combination of factors such as better healthcare, education, employment opportunities, safe and affordable housing, a clean environment, and access to technology. A higher quality of life can lead to increased happiness, reduced stress, and improved physical and mental health. It is an important goal for individuals.

Negative affects of technologies in modern society

Lack of Sleep

Most adults sleep with their cell phones nearby, and so do their children. In fact, four out of five teens sleep with their cell phones in their room, and nearly a third of them sleep with the phones on their beds. Unfortunately, as The Washington Post reports, “The blue light emitted by the screens of mobile devices has been associated with poor sleep, researchers say, but mobile devices also can cause emotional stimulation—through violent games or engaging forms of social media—that also can impair sleep or simply delay the moment when people fall asleep.

Obesity

The increased obesity in children has been well documented. According to the CDC, 18.5% of America’s youth is now considered “obese,” compared to just 5% a few decades earlier. One cause of obesity is a lack of physical activity, and children who overuse electronic devices are less physically active. While play time has been shown to improve mood and increase self-esteem, sedentary activities (such as Internet use) not only lead to decreased physical activity but have been linked to feelings of social isolation and depression.

Cyberbullying

You may already know that cyberbullying is the use of the Internet, cell phones, video game systems or other technology to send or post messages intended to hurt or embarrass someone else. A 2007 Pew Research study found 32% of teens were victims of cyberbullying. Nearly a decade later, a 2016 study by the Cyberbullying Research Center found those numbers were nearly identical. The National Crime Prevention Council puts that number even higher, at 43%.
